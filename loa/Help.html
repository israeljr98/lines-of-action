Lines of Action

Lines of Action (LOA) is a board game invented by Claude Soucie. Java application implemented by Israel Rodriguez.

Rules

  • Play alternates between Black and White, with Black moving first.
  • Each move consists of moving a piece of your color horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
  • A piece must move a number of squares that is exactly equal to the total number of pieces (black and white) on the line along which it chooses to move (the line of action).
  • A piece maybe moved onto an empty square or onto a square occupied by an opposing piece.
  • A piece may jump over friendly pieces (without disturbing them), but may not cross enemy pieces, except one that it captures.
  • A piece may not move off the board, onto another piece of its color, or over an opposing piece.
  • GOAL: Get all of one's pieces into one group of pieces that are connected.

Controlling Play

  • Click a square containing a piece and drag it to where you wish to move it (assuming the move is legal).
  • Under the AI menu, you can select which player you want to turn into an artificial player.
